Aperture 2.0 Product Management

Aperture 2.0 (Marmot) is Komodo Health's AI-powered Medical Affairs platform that transforms how pharma teams identify, engage, and act on Key Opinion Leader intelligence in real time.

Specific company data is masked below due to the open source nature of this project.

About the Problem

Medical Affairs teams at pharmaceutical companies face a critical strategy-to-execution gap. Home office leaders lack real-time patient data and the flexibility to customize KOL scoring for therapeutic area-specific needs, trapping them in rigid, slow-moving consulting cycles that cost $500K+ per year. Meanwhile, field teams miss real-time clinical signals needed to reach "rising stars" and relevant KOLs before the competition defines the narrative.

As the Senior Product Manager, I led the development of Aperture 2.0 to bridge this gap by replacing static, consultant-driven workflows with an AI-first platform that enables continuous discovery, natural language customization, and seamless CRM delivery.

Goals

  • 🤖 AI-Driven KOL Discovery – Continuously monitor scientific shifts and pinpoint "rising stars" by detecting emerging patterns across global research, trials, and congresses
  • 💬 Natural Language Customization – Enable home office teams to instantly customize KOL scoring and tiering using simple, plain-language requests
  • 🌐 360° Field Intelligence – Surface clinical signals across claims, clinical trials, congresses, and scientific publications that siloed legacy sources fail to detect
  • 🚀 Seamless Field Execution – Push prioritized lists and governed insights directly into the MSL's daily workflow via CRM integration

AI Evaluations

Because Aperture 2.0's core value depends on trustworthy AI output — KOL scoring, profile briefs, and natural language customization — I built a structured evaluation program to measure and improve quality before and after every release.

  • 📋 Golden Datasets – Partnered with Medical Affairs subject matter experts to curate reference sets of KOL rankings, profile summaries, and natural language queries with known-correct answers, giving every model change an objective benchmark.
  • ⚖️ LLM-as-Judge Scoring – Automated grading of AI-generated profile briefs and strategic highlights for accuracy, completeness, and tone, with human expert review calibrating the judges to catch drift.
  • 🔎 Groundedness & Hallucination Checks – Verified that every claim in AI output traced back to the Healthcare Map, publications, trials, or congress data — a hard requirement for a product whose users must defend KOL lists internally.
  • 🔁 Regression Evals in the Release Cycle – Ran the eval suite on every prompt, model, and retrieval change so quality regressions were caught before customers saw them, not after.

Failures & Lessons Learned

  • ⚖️ Balancing AI Transparency with Simplicity – Early iterations of the AI-generated profiles were too opaque, making it difficult for Medical Affairs leaders to defend KOL lists internally. We had to invest significantly in making the AI logic explainable without overwhelming users with complexity.
  • 🔗 Field Adoption Requires More Than Great Features – Building seamless CRM delivery was technically sound, but field teams needed more onboarding and change management support than anticipated. We learned that workflow transformation requires both the tool and the training.
  • ⏳ Scope Creep in Natural Language – The natural language customization feature attracted significant scope creep as stakeholders saw its potential. We had to ruthlessly prioritize the most impactful use cases for launch and defer advanced scenarios to subsequent releases.

Key Capabilities

  • 🔍 Rising Star Identification – AI continuously monitors patient insights, clinical trials, and congress data to detect emerging KOLs before they appear on competitors' radar, providing a multi-month head start on scientific engagement.
  • 📋 Deep Contextual Profiles – Instantly generate comprehensive HCP and HCO profiles with rich engagement data including referral networks, industry payments, clinical trends, scientific impact, and network influence.
