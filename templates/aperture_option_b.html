Aperture 2.0: AI-Powered Medical Affairs

Replacing static, consultant-driven Medical Affairs workflows with real-time AI discovery, natural language customization, and seamless CRM delivery.

Specific company data is masked below due to the open source nature of this project.

The Problem

Medical Affairs teams at pharmaceutical companies face a critical strategy-to-execution gap. Home office leaders lack real-time patient data and the flexibility to customize KOL scoring for therapeutic area-specific needs, trapping them in rigid, slow-moving consulting cycles that cost $500K+ per year. Meanwhile, field teams miss real-time clinical signals needed to reach "rising stars" and relevant KOLs before the competition defines the narrative.

As the Senior Product Manager, I led the development of Aperture 2.0 to bridge this gap with an AI-first platform for continuous discovery, natural language customization, and seamless CRM delivery.

What I Built

  • 🤖 AI-Driven KOL Discovery – Continuous monitoring of scientific shifts to pinpoint "rising stars" across global research, trials, and congresses
  • 💬 Natural Language Customization – Instant, plain-language customization of KOL scoring and tiering
  • 🌐 360° Field Intelligence – Clinical signals across claims, trials, congresses, and publications that siloed legacy sources miss
  • 🚀 Seamless Field Execution – Prioritized lists and governed insights pushed directly into the MSL's CRM workflow

The Analysis Canvas: natural language chat drives regional segmentation and geographic KOL mapping.

AI Evaluations

Because the product's core value depends on trustworthy AI output, I built a structured evaluation program that ran before and after every release:

  • 📋 Golden Datasets – Reference sets curated with Medical Affairs SMEs as objective benchmarks for every model change
  • ⚖️ LLM-as-Judge Scoring – Automated grading of AI briefs, calibrated by human expert review
  • 🔎 Groundedness Checks – Every claim traced back to the Healthcare Map, publications, trials, or congress data
  • 🔁 Regression Evals – The eval suite ran on every prompt, model, and retrieval change

Failures & Lessons Learned

  • ⚖️ Balancing AI Transparency with Simplicity – Early AI-generated profiles were too opaque to defend internally; we invested heavily in explainability without overwhelming users.
  • 🔗 Field Adoption Requires More Than Great Features – Field teams needed far more onboarding and change management than anticipated. The tool alone isn't the transformation.
  • ⏳ Scope Creep in Natural Language – NL customization attracted heavy scope creep; we ruthlessly prioritized launch use cases and deferred the rest.
