The Problem

Medical Affairs teams at pharmaceutical companies face a critical strategy-to-execution gap. Home office leaders lack real-time patient data and the flexibility to customize KOL scoring for therapeutic area-specific needs, trapping them in rigid, slow-moving consulting cycles that cost $500K+ per year. Meanwhile, field teams miss real-time clinical signals needed to reach "rising stars" and relevant KOLs before the competition defines the narrative.

As the Senior Product Manager, I led the development of Aperture 2.0 to bridge this gap by replacing static, consultant-driven workflows with an AI-first platform that enables continuous discovery, natural language customization, and seamless CRM delivery.

Goals

🤖 AI-Driven KOL Discovery — Continuously monitor scientific shifts and pinpoint "rising stars" across global research, trials, and congresses.
💬 Natural Language Customization — Let home office teams instantly customize KOL scoring and tiering in plain language.
🌐 360° Field Intelligence — Surface clinical signals across claims, trials, congresses, and publications that siloed legacy sources miss.
🚀 Seamless Field Execution — Push prioritized lists and governed insights directly into the MSL's daily CRM workflow.

The Product

Aperture 2.0 replaces static consulting-driven workflows with real-time, AI-driven intelligence across the full HCP engagement lifecycle: rising star identification, deep contextual profiles with referral networks and scientific impact, an interactive Analysis Canvas with tiered segmentation, and a Discover feed of AI-curated congress, trial, and publication intelligence.

Development Timeline

  • Identified the strategy-to-execution gap through extensive Medical Affairs customer research and competitive analysis
  • Designed the AI-driven discovery engine to continuously monitor patient insights, clinical trials, and congress data
  • Built natural language customization to replace expensive consulting workflows for KOL scoring and tiering
  • Developed the Analysis Canvas with deep contextual HCP profiles, geographic mapping, and tiered segmentation
  • Launched CRM integration to push prioritized lists and network insights directly into field team workflows

AI Evaluations

Because Aperture 2.0's core value depends on trustworthy AI output, I built a structured evaluation program to measure and improve quality before and after every release.

📋 Golden Datasets — Curated reference sets with Medical Affairs SMEs so every model change had an objective benchmark.
⚖️ LLM-as-Judge Scoring — Automated grading of AI briefs for accuracy and tone, calibrated by human expert review.
🔎 Groundedness Checks — Verified every claim traced back to the Healthcare Map, publications, trials, or congress data.
🔁 Regression Evals — Ran the eval suite on every prompt, model, and retrieval change to catch regressions pre-release.

Failures & Lessons Learned

  • ⚖️ Balancing AI Transparency with Simplicity — Early AI-generated profiles were too opaque to defend internally. We invested heavily in making the logic explainable without overwhelming users.
  • 🔗 Field Adoption Requires More Than Great Features — Seamless CRM delivery was technically sound, but field teams needed far more onboarding and change management than anticipated.
  • ⏳ Scope Creep in Natural Language — The NL customization feature attracted heavy scope creep; we ruthlessly prioritized launch use cases and deferred advanced scenarios.
